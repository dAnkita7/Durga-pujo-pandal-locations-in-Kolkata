The Story of Rama

In some parts of India, especially in the northern regions, Durga Puja coincides with the festival of Navaratri. During this time, Durga is worshipped as a manifestation of Goddess Parvati. Goddess Parvatis' all nine forms are worshiped During these days. In some manuscripts it is said that Lord Rama prayed to Parvati to grant him the power to defeat King of Lanka Ravana. Thus, Durga is seen as an ally of Lord Rama in his battle against evil. Dasami As DASHEERA celebrates the victory of Lord Rama over the demon King Ravana. Effigies of Ravana are burnt in open fields, and it symbolizes the triumph of good over evil. In bengali culture this story is interpreted as, in ealier time Durga puja was held during 'Vasant' (Spring). But during battle between Lord Rama and King Ravana, to seek help from goddess Durga,Lord Rama performed a rigorous ritual in 'Sarat' (Autum). Being pleased with Lord Rama's offering Goddess Durga blessed him that he will win against King Ravana. That;s why many a times Durga puja during autumn is called 'SARADIA'.
